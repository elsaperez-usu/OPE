What is the OPE?

The Oral Proficiency Examination (OPE) tests your ability to perform the three core modes of court interpreting. It leads to Certified Court Interpreter status — the highest credential in Utah Courts. You must score 70%+ on each part independently.

Part 1 — Sight Translation

Read a written legal document and render it orally in the target language in real time. Both EN→ES and ES→EN.

Part 2 — Consecutive

Listen to a complete utterance, take notes, then deliver the full rendition in the target language.

Part 3 — Simultaneous

Render source language into target language in real time with minimal lag (chuchotage in court).

Most common reasons candidates fail
All modes
  • Omissions — leaving out any content, even filler words
  • Register breaks — changing speaker's formality level
  • Adding explanations — interpreters do not clarify
  • False cognates — embarrassed ≠ embarazada
  • Number errors — double-check all figures
Mode-specific
  • Sight: word-for-word literal reading
  • Sight: not completing within time limit
  • Consecutive: starting before speaker finishes
  • Consecutive: losing details due to poor notes
  • Simultaneous: ear-voice span greater than 5 seconds

Register guide — match the speaker exactly
Judge / Formal Legal

Highly formal. Long sentences. Passive voice. No contractions. "Usted" always. Use: "de conformidad con," "sírvase," "en virtud de."

Attorney / Semi-formal

Professional but direct. May use rhetorical questions. Technical legal terms mixed with plain language. Controlled emotion.

Witness / Everyday

Informal to colloquial. May include slang, hesitations, incomplete sentences. Reproduce exactly — do NOT correct the speaker.
